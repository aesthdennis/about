Thus be Dionysius

Denis B. Daniel

Thus be Dionysius is an amalgamation of philosophical musings, introspective ideals, and a departure from traditional conventions, all through the lens of the protagonist, Dionysius — an embodiment of the author's unconscious desires, characterized by contradiction, neurosis, and delusion. 

Dionysius, not to be confused with the Greek God "Dionysus”, is a metaphysical individual that had been hiding within his mind for all of his existence, however, one day, a desire for understanding the external world had struck — a new hope that seeks a deeper comprehension of the world that surrounds him.

This fictional exploration of metaphysics is not bound by linear objectives but rather flows as a continuous stream of consciousness, encompassing the author's aspiration for a heightened perception and understanding.
 

 

Sempiternus: Rebirth

Denis B. Daniel

Transcending metaphysical dogmas and incorporating cultural Christianity within the individual's framework of intuition and logical dependencies.

Offering a departure from conventional theology, collectivist metaphysics, herd-minded mentalities, presenting a version of Christianity that empowers the individual.

Emphasizing the idea of overmen, who take charge of their lives independently, in an iconoclastic manner.
